Other datasets

1 What other datasets are there?

There exist thousands of data sets that you can freely analyse. This section covers a few of the major ones for education, or that might fruitfully be combined with educational data sets such as PISA.

2 Gender equality indices

There are several international datasets for studying gender equality. These have been used by researchers to look at the impact of gender equality on student attitudes and outcomes. For example Stoet and Geary (2018) use the Global Gender Gap Index (GGGI) too look at PISA science self-efficacy and science outcomes for females.

2.1 Global Gender Gap Index (GGGI)

The world economic forum produces the Global Gender Gap Index (GGGI), this index combines female and male outcomes on Economic participation and opportunity, educational attainment, health and survival, and political empowerment.

Reports for: 2022, 2021, 2020,2018,2017,2016, 2015

It has proven difficult to find the 2015 dataset used by Stoet & Geary, the 2013 dataset is here

2.2 United Nations

The UN reports on two gender specific indexes:

Gender Inequality Index (GII)

The Gender Inequality Index is a index incorporating data on reproductive health, empowerment and the labour market. Values range from 0 - full equality for men and women, to 0, full inequality.

Gender Development Index (GDI)

The Gender Development Index measures inequalities in human development, combining data on female and male life expectancy, years of schooling and earned income. Values of 1 indicate equality, with values of less than 1 showing males performing better, and values over 1 showing females doing better.

Downloads for the GII and GDI are here

3 UNESCO

UNESCO produces a number of open data sets of relevance to education:

  • STEM degree entry by country

The UNESCO Institute of Statistics data browser allows you to create data sets including:

  • Demographics of teachers
  • Mean years of schooling
  • Graduation ratios
  • Out of school children and adolescents
  • Educational expenditure
  • Illiteracy rates
  • Survival (in schooling) rate by grade

5 LaNA - Literacy and Numeracy Assessment

“Rooted in the comprehensive assessment frameworks of TIMSS and PIRLS, IEA’s LaNA is designed to produce national data for monitoring foundational reading and mathematics and learning goals in participating countries at the end of primary school” - https://www.iea.nl/studies/iea/LaNA

6 China Family Panel Studies

Run by Peking University, the CFPS focuses on China and includes data on social, economic, education and health. You must register to access the data sets.

7 UNICEF

Multiple Indicator Cluster Surveys, Household survey, looking at education and social characteristics of children and adults around the world. To access the data you need to register with UNICEF, and approval for data access can take several days https://mics.unicef.org/surveys

8 Office of National Statistics (in the UK)

The UK ONS site contains a number of general data sets related to the UK, for example, census data, including

The site includes a number of education related data sets including:

  • Numbers of young people not in education, employment or training (NEETs)
  • Covid in schools surveys

9 OECD

The OECD offers over 1,600 data sets on the countries that it covers, including data on patents, education, mortality and the environment. You can combine these data sets with PISA etc, to explore national trends.

Of interest might be the Survey on Social and Emotional Skills (SSES) which is taken by children aged 10 and 15.

To access the OECD database collection use https://stats.oecd.org/. There is catalogue of datasets

Whilst it is possible to access the data sets directly you might find it easier to use the R OECD package that converts the sdmx files in R dataframes:
